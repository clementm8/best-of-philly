My first Philly CheeseSteak Pizza

The first time I tried a Philly cheesesteak pizza, I was at a restaurant watching the 2016 NBA Finals with friends. I vividly remember the server bringing the pizza to our table and how the smell caught everyone's attention. As I took a bite, the crispy, golden crust gave way to a rich layer of melted provolone and mozzarella cheese, that covered tender strips of well-seasoned beef. Each mouthful was a perfect mix of flavors: the juicy steak paired wonderfully with caramelized onions and bell peppers, all topped with a zesty marinara sauce that had just the right amount of spice. The smell was mouthwatering, with hints of garlic and pepper that made the whole experience even better. It was an amazing combination of two classic dishes that completely exceeded my expectations.

What's in a Philly Cheesesteak Pizza

A Philly cheesesteak pizza combines the classic flavors of a Philly cheesesteak sandwich with a pizza base, creating a delicious fusion dish. The pizza is typically topped with thinly sliced ribeye steak or beef, which is often sautéed with onions and green bell peppers to add savory depth. Mozzarella and provolone cheeses are commonly used to melt over the toppings, giving a creamy, stretchy texture. Some versions include mushrooms and other vegetables like banana peppers for extra flavor. The base can be a traditional tomato sauce or a white sauce, depending on preference, and sometimes drizzles of cheese whiz or garlic butter are added for that authentic Philly cheesesteak touch. The result is a hearty, flavorful pizza that pays homage to the beloved Philadelphia sandwich.

History of Philly Cheesesteak Pizza

The Philly cheesesteak pizza is a modern twist on Philadelphia's iconic cheesesteak sandwich, which dates back to the 1930s when Pat and Harry Olivieri first created the sandwich with thinly sliced steak and onions. Over time, cheese was added, and the sandwich became a Philadelphia favorite. As pizza grew in popularity across the U.S., pizzerias began experimenting with regional dishes, leading to the creation of the Philly cheesesteak pizza in the late 20th century. This pizza combines the essential cheesesteak ingredients—steak, onions, peppers, and melted cheese—on a pizza crust, offering a new way to enjoy the flavors of the classic sandwich. Today, its a popular choice, especially in the Philadelphia area, blending the city's beloved sandwich with the comfort of pizza..
